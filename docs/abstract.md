# abstract

This study investigates whether weather-induced mood affects investors’ speculative demand for lottery-like stocks in the Korean equity market. Using detailed meteorological data to construct a daily weather pleasantness index and matching it to firms’ headquarter locations, we examine how pleasant weather influences retail investors’ trading behavior. We measure lottery characteristics using MAX, return skewness, idiosyncratic volatility, and price levels, and use investor-type transaction data from the Korea Exchange to capture retail demand. Our results show that pleasant weather significantly increases retail investors’ net buying of lottery-like stocks, leading to stronger short-term price pressure on high-MAX firms. These effects subsequently reverse, consistent with sentiment-driven overpricing. The impact is more pronounced for smaller and less liquid firms, where limits to arbitrage are tighter. Overall, the findings highlight weather-driven mood as an important determinant of speculative trading and the pricing of lottery characteristics in an emerging market context.
